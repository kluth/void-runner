# MANUAL: THE GLOBAL GRID & ROUTING

## I. THE GRID: AN ARCHITECTURE OF CONTROL
The Global Grid is not a "cyberspace" of neon lights and digital fantasies. It is a brutalist infrastructure of monolithic data-pipelines, a cold and unforgiving expanse of logic gates and hard-coded protocols. In VOID_RUN, the Grid is your playground and your executioner. It is a strictly hierarchical network managed by the Centralized Authority, designed to facilitate the flow of capital and information while ruthlessly pruning "anomalies."

As a Runner, you do not navigate the Grid; you infiltrate its inefficiencies. You are a ghost in the machine, exploiting the very routing protocols that maintain order.

## II. ROUTING FUNDAMENTALS
All communication within the Grid is packet-based, moving through a series of interconnected Nodes. To reach a target—be it a secure server, a corporate database, or a private terminal—your signal must jump across multiple subnets.

### 1. Subnet Jumping
Each hop increases your "Signal Lag." While the Authority's hardware can process packets in nanoseconds, your bootleg deck will struggle. If your lag exceeds 500ms, your connection becomes unstable, making you vulnerable to packet-loss and Trace detection. Plan your route through low-traffic relay nodes to maintain a clean uplink.

### 2. Packet Sniffing & Interception
Routing isn't just about moving; it's about seeing. By sitting on a high-traffic junction, you can sniff passing packets to harvest credentials, encryption keys, or lore fragments. However, "Passive Sniffing" is a misnomer. Every byte you intercept generates a tiny amount of noise. Too much noise triggers the node's local watchdog.

## III. SECURITY LAYERS: NODES & ICE
Every node in the Global Grid is protected by layers of Intrusion Countermeasure Electronics (ICE).
- **Gatekeeper Nodes:** These are the checkpoints. They require valid handshakes or a brutal "Forced-Route" exploit.
- **Sentinel ICE:** These don't stop you; they watch you. They append tracking metadata to your packets, slowly building a profile of your location.
- **Black ICE:** Lethal defensive software. If you fail a routing handshake at a Black ICE node, expect a localized hardware surge that will fry your deck and, potentially, your nervous system.

## IV. TACTICAL ADVICE
- **Diversionary Routing:** Before hitting your primary target, set up three low-priority connections to unrelated subnets. This dilutes the Authority's trace algorithms, buying you precious seconds.
- **The "Dead Man's Switch":** Always have an exit route mapped. If the Grid turns red, disconnect immediately. A "Hard-Drop" is better than a "Brain-Burn."

**REMEMBER:** The Grid does not want you there. It is a machine of absolute order. Your existence is a syntax error.
