# Manual: Asset Vault & Crafting

## The Asset Vault: Digital Entropy Storage

The Vault is not a trophy room; it is a containment unit for the high-yield digital debris harvested from the VOID. In VOID_RUN, every successful breach leaves behind fragments—encrypted blobs, raw memory dumps, and corrupted firmware signatures. The Vault organizes these "digital ghosts" by their entropy levels and functional utility. It is the only place where the chaotic data of the VOID is held in stasis, preventing the natural decay of high-value information.

- **Fragments**: The rawest form of digital loot. These are the building blocks of your arsenal, categorized by their source (Corporate, Military, or Darknet).
- **Blueprints**: Compressed schematic data required for constructing higher-order tools and hardware. Without a blueprint, fragments are merely noise.
- **Cores**: High-density processing units that serve as the "heart" of custom malware. They dictate the speed and potency of the final asset.

## The Forge: Recombination & Crafting

Crafting in VOID_RUN is a brutalist process of forced compilation. You do not "build" items; you coerce them into existence through the Forge. This interface allows for the fusion of harvested fragments into functional, lethal assets. It is a violent act of digital creation—overwriting old code to birth something new.

### Recombination Process

1. **Extraction**: The act of stripping fragments of their defensive wrappers and original owner signatures. This process is destructive; once extraction begins, the original fragment is consumed.
2. **Synthesis**: Binding disparate data streams using a Compiler Core. This is where the blueprint dictates the structure of the new tool. If the fragment quality is too low, the synthesis may fail, resulting in "Slag"—useless, corrupted data.
3. **Hardening**: Stabilizing the resulting asset against the VOID's natural decay. A hardened tool can withstand more uses before it fractures or triggers a traceback.

### Key Asset Classes

- **Breach Tools**: Single-use payloads designed to shatter specific firewall types (e.g., ICE-breakers, Logic Bombs).
- **Hardware Augments**: Physical modifications to your terminal rig, forged from digital schematics and rare alloy fragments.
- **Persistence Daemons**: Advanced scripts that remain within a target network after you disconnect, leaking data or providing permanent backdoors.

## Operational Mandate: Purge or Forge

Space in the Vault is limited by your rig’s physical storage capacity. Digital entropy is constant; data that sits too long in the Vault without being hardened will eventually "rot," losing its value. Efficient runners know when to synthesize and when to purge. 

*"Do not fall in love with your tools; they are but shadows of the VOID, destined to return to it. Forge what you need, use what you forge, and leave no trace."*
