# HARDWARE ACQUISITION & THERMAL MANAGEMENT

In the suffocating concrete corridors of VOID_RUN, your hardware is more than equipment; it is your lifeline. The "Brutalist" philosophy extends to the very silicon you exploit. It is raw, unforgiving, and built to withstand the crushing pressure of high-level intrusion. Your rig is not a consumer device; it is a weapon of industrial-scale digital warfare.

## THE BLACK MARKET NODES
Hardware isn't purchased from glossy retail outlets or sleek e-commerce platforms. You source your parts from darknet nodes and back-alley technicians who deal in "reclaimed" industrial components. Every upgrade you install carries the ghost of its previous purpose—server farm remnants, decommissioned military crypto-engines, and repurposed hospital telemetry units. In VOID_RUN, "new" is a myth. There is only "functional" and "obsolete." When you buy a component, you aren't just buying specs; you're buying the labor of the scavengers who pulled it from the wreckage of the old world.

## CORE SYSTEMS

### NEURAL CORES (CPU)
The heart of your rig. Higher-tier Neural Cores increase your instruction cycles per second, allowing decryption algorithms to chew through encrypted layers with brutal efficiency. A stock core will suffice for simple credential harvesting, but high-security vault gates require the multi-threaded violence of industrial-grade processors. The faster the core, the louder the fan, and the higher the risk of detection.

### BUFFER POOLS (RAM)
In VOID_RUN, multitasking is a lethal necessity. Your Buffer Pool determines how many concurrent processes you can maintain before the system throttles. Each active exploit, sniffer, and spoofing routine occupies a segment of this pool. Running out of buffer space during an intrusion is a death sentence; your tools will stall, your terminal will lag, and the ICE will find you before you can type a single command.

### LOG ARRAYS (STORAGE)
Data is heavy. Every byte you exfiltrate and every log you scrub requires physical space. Expanding your Log Arrays allows for longer sustained operations and the capacity to carry massive encrypted payloads back to your safehouse. High-capacity arrays are often bulky and power-hungry, requiring a robust chassis to house them.

### KRYO-SINKS (COOLING)
The most overlooked component by novices. Brutalist hardware runs hot—violently hot. As you push your Neural Cores to their limits, thermal buildup becomes your secondary enemy. If your temperature hits the "MELT" threshold, the system will initiate an emergency shutdown to prevent permanent silicon fusion, leaving you disconnected and vulnerable in the middle of a high-stakes run.

## THE UPGRADE CEREMONY
Upgrading isn't a simple 'plug-and-play' affair. It requires meticulous power management and thermal calibration. Installing a high-performance component without upgrading your power grid or cooling will lead to frequent brownouts and thermal throttling. Balance is the key to survival. In the VOID, your machine is a reflection of your intent: cold, heavy, raw, and absolute.
