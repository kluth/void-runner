# MANUAL: THREAT DETECTION & SYSTEM HEAT

## [0x00] THE FRICTION OF INTRUSION
In VOID_RUN, you are never invisible. You are an anomaly in a streamlined flow of data. Every action—every decrypted packet, every forced entry, every unauthorized query—generates **System Heat**. Heat is the digital friction between your deck and the target’s security protocols. If you move too fast or too loudly, the friction ignites.

## [0x01] UNDERSTANDING HEAT DYNAMICS
Heat is represented as a persistent logarithmic gauge on your HUD. It does not dissipate naturally; it must be managed. 

*   **Static Heat:** Generated by maintaining a connection. The longer you stay, the more the host's background heuristics notice your presence.
*   **Active Heat:** Spikes caused by aggressive actions (e.g., `BRUTE_FORCE`, `SYSCALL_OVERRIDE`).
*   **Trace Progress:** When Heat exceeds the 75% threshold, the host initiates a **Deep Trace**. This is a non-linear process that attempts to bridge the gap between the virtual node and your physical terminal.

## [0x02] THREAT LEVELS & RESPONSE
The system's response to your Heat is tiered. Ignore these warnings at your own peril:

1.  **Tier 1: [BLUE] Passive Analysis.** The host increases encryption complexity. Decryption times are extended by 15%.
2.  **Tier 2: [YELLOW] Active Scouting.** ICMP "ping" sweeps become frequent. You must manually dodge these or risk a 20% Heat spike.
3.  **Tier 3: [ORANGE] Sentinel Deployment.** Defensive sub-routines (Black ICE) begin scanning your memory sectors. Active purging is required.
4.  **Tier 4: [RED] Terminal Lockout.** The host attempts to sever the uplink. Failure to bypass results in deck fried and save-state corruption.

## [0x03] MITIGATION & COOLING
A master runner knows when to push and when to vent. Use the following protocols to manage your signature:

*   **SIGNAL_DITHER:** Randomizes your packet headers. Reduces Passive Heat accumulation but increases latency.
*   **HEATSINK_DUMP:** Diverts digital friction into temporary buffer files. These files must be deleted manually (`rm -rf /tmp/heat_*`) or they will eventually "leak" back into your main gauge.
*   **GHOST_ECHO:** Creates a dummy connection to a neighboring node. 50% of Heat spikes are redirected to the echo until it is destroyed by the host.

## [0x04] THE TERMINAL CONSEQUENCE
If your Heat reaches 100%, the "Terminal Condition" is met. The host executes a `HARD_SHUTDOWN`. This is not a soft disconnect. Your hardware will suffer physical degradation, and your current mission progress is permanently purged. In the world of VOID_RUN, silence is survival.
