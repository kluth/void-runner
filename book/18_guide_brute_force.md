# GUIDE: BRUTE FORCE CODE BREAKING

## 0x01: PHILOSOPHY OF THE HAMMER
In the digital architecture of VOID_RUN, elegance is a luxury for those with time. When the subtle path—the exploit, the back door, the social engineering—is sealed, you resort to the Hammer. Brute forcing is not an act of cleverness; it is an act of industrial-scale aggression. It is the process of systematic exhaustion, where every possible permutation of a cryptographic key or password is tested until the lock yields. In the brutalist corridors of the Void, we do not ask for entry; we demand it through sheer computational weight. To brute force is to acknowledge that every security measure is merely a delay, a mathematical hurdle that can be pulverized by a sufficiently powerful engine of logic. It is the ultimate expression of the Runner's will over the machine's static defenses.

## 0x02: ENTROPY AND COLLISIONS
The target's security is defined by its entropy—the randomness of its keyspace. A 4-digit PIN is a joke, a 10-millisecond exercise. An 8-character alphanumeric string is a wall. A 256-bit AES key is a mountain. To break these, your rig must generate "collisions" at a rate that outpaces the target’s lockout timers. 

Your terminal interface will display the **Keyspace Map**. Watch the density of the bit-fields. As your processors churn through the ranges, the map will shift from static grey to flickering amber. When you hit a collision, the system registers a "Partial Match" (PM). Use PMs to narrow your search radius. Brute forcing is the art of turning infinite possibilities into a finite, slaughterable list.

## 0x03: HARDWARE VS. THERMALS
Brute forcing is the most resource-intensive operation a Runner can perform. It demands every cycle from your Neural Core and every watt from your Power Grid. 
- **Parallelization:** Deploying multiple "Worker Threads" increases your hash rate but divides your cooling capacity.
- **Thermal Throttling:** As your hardware hits the 95°C threshold, clock speeds will drop. A throttled rig is a dead rig. If your internal components melt, the brute force fails, and you lose the hardware.
- **Overclocking:** You can push beyond factory limits to shave minutes off a 128-bit crack, but the risk of "Bit Flip" errors increases, potentially corrupting the very password you are trying to find.

## 0x04: THE COST OF NOISE
Brute forcing is *loud*. Every failed attempt is a recorded heartbeat on the target’s Intrusion Countermeasure Electronics (ICE) monitor. While a stealthy probe might go unnoticed for hours, a brute force attack is a siren. The moment you initiate the sequence, the "Trace Timer" begins. You are not just fighting the math; you are fighting the clock. If the ICE completes its trace before you find the key, your location is burned.

## 0x05: THE BRUTE'S CREED
Do not look for the key. Become the door-breaker. If the wall is too thick, bring a bigger hammer. In VOID_RUN, math is the only absolute law—and math can be beaten with enough heat and enough time. Remember: the machine does not feel pain, but it can be broken by the relentless pressure of a million wrong answers delivered in a single second. Your victory is not in the "Aha!" moment of discovery, but in the final, shuddering collapse of the target's resistance. Stand amidst the debris of the cracked cipher and claim your prize. The Void honors the strong.
