# Lore: The Blue Team

In the asymmetric warfare of the VOID, if the Runners are the lightning—brief, violent, and unpredictable—then the Blue Team is the mountain. They are the immovable force, the cold logic of the status quo, and the silent architects of the walls that define the digital horizon. To a runner, the Blue Team is often perceived as an abstract antagonist, a series of hardening encryption gates and lethal ICE. But the reality is far more clinical, and far more terrifying.

## The Sentinels of the Grid

The Blue Team is not a single entity, but a collective term for the defensive divisions of the Great Megacorps—Aegis Security, Hyperion Systems, and the dreaded Obsidian Watch. These aren't just IT departments; they are paramilitary organizations operating in a purely digital theater. Their mandate is simple: Total Data Integrity. In the brutalist logic of the corporations, a single leaked byte is a breach of reality itself.

Unlike the runners, who operate from the shadows with jury-rigged uplinks and stolen bandwidth, the Blue Team operates from "Fortresses"—massive, multi-layered data centers cooled by liquid nitrogen and protected by physical layers of armed security. They don't just react to intrusions; they curate the digital environment to make intrusion impossible.

## Proactive Countermeasures: The "Deep Freeze"

When a Blue Team operator detects a runner, their response is rarely a simple "lockout." They employ "Deep-Freeze" protocols—a method of isolating a network segment and flooding it with high-entropy noise to trap the runner's neural uplink. While the runner is trapped in the digital sludge, the Blue Team’s "Bloodhounds" (specialized AI-tracking subroutines) trace the physical origin of the signal.

Their arsenal includes:

1.  **Passive Sentry-Nodes:** Silent monitors that record every packet without interference, waiting for the runner to commit to a deep-dive before closing the trap.
2.  **Psych-ICE:** Specialized intrusion countermeasures designed to exploit the neural vulnerabilities of the runner’s uplink, inducing vertigo, panic, or temporary paralysis.
3.  **The Black-Hole Protocol:** A scorched-earth defensive measure where a server node is intentionally purged, destroying the data but incinerating any connected uplinks in the process.

## The Cost of Vigilance

The human operators of the Blue Team are often as scarred as the runners they hunt. Known as "Sentries," these individuals are permanently "slotted" into the corporate grid, their neural uplinks tuned for maximum sensitivity to anomalies. They live in a world of constant, low-grade sensory input, their minds integrated with the very architecture they defend. For a Sentry, the corporate grid isn't just a job; it is their home, their skin, and their religion. They don't hate the runners—they simply view them as a virus to be purged from the system.

In the VOID, the Blue Team is the wall that never sleeps. And eventually, every runner hits the wall.
